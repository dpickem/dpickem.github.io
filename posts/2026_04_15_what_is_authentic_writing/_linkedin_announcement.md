## LinkedIn post

"What Is Authentic Writing (in the Age of AI)?"

I've been noticing that writing - not coding - has become my biggest bottleneck. I have plenty of ideas, tutorials, and observations I want to share, but producing a polished blog post still takes a lot of time. AI assistants can crank out code and iterate on designs, but when it comes to prose they either generate slop that sounds like a sales pitch or completely dry design-doc-style text I barely want to read myself.

So I tried treating writing like software engineering.

At work, I've built a compounding loop for AI-assisted code reviews: formalize repo-specific guidelines in a CONTRIBUTING.md, feed them into a review agent, iterate, and fold anything the loop misses back into the guidelines. Each cycle gets better (and the reviews are ones I'm comfortable posting under my own name).

I'm want to apply the same pattern to writing. So I had my AI agent analyze 13 of my manually written 2025 blog posts and extract my voice - sentence constructions, characteristic phrases, structural motifs, vocabulary, even my signature sign-off. The resulting VOICE.md file acts as a style guide for my writing, similar to what CONTRIBUTING.md does for my code.

The workflow I want to set up will roughly looks like this:
→ Seed a post with raw ideas and a topic
→ Let AI draft and structure, applying VOICE.md
→ Review everything personally (call me old-fashioned, but I review everything I ship)
→ Iterate until it sounds like me
→ Feed improvements back into VOICE.md

Much like the Turing test, if you can't tell whether a post was written by me or my AI assistant, does it really matter? The outcome is the same after all.

Blog post: https://danielpickem.com/posts/2026_04_15_what_is_authentic_writing/
